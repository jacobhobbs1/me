About me

I recently completed my PhD at Open Lab, Newcastle University, where I researched how augmented reality can be meaningfully integrated into medical imaging workflows. My work combined interviews and workshops with radiologists, surgeons, and other clinical stakeholders across the North East of England, exploring how AR can enhance efficiency, support clinical decision-making, and improve medical training. This research led to publications in JMIR XR and Spatial Computing and MDPI Informatics, the latter presenting a scoping literature review on the perceptual challenges of head-mounted displays with implications that extend well beyond healthcare.

With my PhD now complete, I am moving into the product industry as an Assistant Digital Producer at Enigma Interactive, where I bring a research-grounded perspective to the design and production of digital experiences. My academic background in human-computer interaction and extended reality informs how I think about building technology that fits into real human practice — not just in theory, but in the messy, high-stakes environments where it actually has to work.

Alongside my research, I have taught on undergraduate modules in HCI and interaction design at Newcastle University, and I hold a BSc in Computer Science from the same institution. My final dissertation explored the development of digital twins, including a working digital twin of an F1Tenth car. Prior to my PhD, I completed a placement with the EPiC research group, where I built a command-line interface simulator to support student learning — an early project that shaped my interest in how people learn through and with technology.
