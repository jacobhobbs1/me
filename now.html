About me

I'm a researcher and teaching assistant at Newcastle University, working at the intersection of human-computer interaction, interaction design, and extended reality. My research focuses on how clinicians — particularly radiologists — can work with augmented reality systems in ways that genuinely fit their practice, not just their workflows on paper.

I'm interested in the gap between what technology promises and what it actually does for people under real conditions. That interest runs through my research methods too: I prefer talking to people, watching them work, and taking seriously what they say.

Outside of research, I teach on the undergraduate HCI and Interaction Design module, which I enjoy more than I probably admit. Thinking about how to explain things well keeps me honest about whether I actually understand them.

Right now

Writing

AR in Radiology — Paper submission

Finalising a paper on AR interactions in radiology for Virtual Worlds (MDPI). Seven interviews with practising radiologists and registrars, analysed through reflexive thematic analysis and NASA-TLX. Framing the findings around an "AR-First world" concept.

Teaching

HCI & Interaction Design

Teaching assistant on the undergraduate module at Newcastle University, supporting students through interaction design projects and studio crits.

Research

Extended reality in clinical contexts

Continuing to explore how XR devices — HoloLens 2, Meta Quest 3 — fit (or don't) into radiology workflows. Interested in developing better evaluation frameworks for AR in practice.
